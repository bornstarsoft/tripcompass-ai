A Seoul to Tokyo 4 day itinerary needs sharper choices than many short Japan trips. Tokyo has enough food, shopping, museums, neighborhoods, and pop culture for a much longer stay, so the best short plan groups each day by area.

This page is planning guidance only. It does not include real-time prices, live availability, visa rules, opening hours, safety guarantees, or booking guarantees. For a personalized destination shortlist, start with the [TripCompass AI finder](/#destination-finder).

## Who this page is for

This page is for Korea-based travelers choosing Tokyo for a focused 3 night or 4 day trip. It is useful if you want a first Tokyo plan without trying to cover every famous district.

## Best-fit travelers

| Traveler type | Tokyo fit | Practical note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| First-time Japan travelers | Tokyo offers the widest range of neighborhoods and experiences. | Plan by area to avoid crossing the city too often. |
| Shopping and pop culture travelers | Shibuya, Harajuku, Akihabara, Ginza, and themed districts can fit different interests. | Choose only a few anchors per day. |
| Food-focused travelers | Dining variety is broad across price styles and neighborhoods. | Reservation and closing-day checks matter. |
| Couples or solo travelers | Tokyo supports flexible, themed days. | Leave buffers for transit and weather. |

## Suggested trip length

- Day 1: arrive, settle near your main transit area, and keep dinner nearby.
- Day 2: choose a west-side route such as Shibuya, Harajuku, Omotesando, or Shinjuku.
- Day 3: choose an east-side or culture route such as Asakusa, Ueno, Akihabara, Ginza, or Tokyo Station.
- Day 4: keep one short neighborhood, shopping buffer, or airport-friendly plan.

## Budget considerations

Tokyo budgets depend heavily on hotel area and daily movement. A room near your planned neighborhoods can reduce wasted time. A cheaper hotel farther out may be fine if transit is simple, but it can become tiring when the itinerary crosses the city.

- Tokyo is large; distant neighborhoods on the same day can waste time.
- Attraction hours, restaurant hours, seasonal closures, and reservation rules should be checked close to travel.
- Visa, passport, and entry requirements should be verified from official sources.
- Weather and major events can change crowd patterns.
