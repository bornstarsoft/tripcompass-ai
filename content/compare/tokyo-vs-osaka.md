Tokyo and Osaka both work well for a first Japan trip, but they solve different travel problems.

## Quick Recommendation

Choose Tokyo if you want the widest range of neighborhoods, restaurants, museums, shopping, and pop culture. Choose Osaka if you want an easier base for food, nightlife, and Kansai day trips to Kyoto or Nara.

| Factor | Tokyo | Osaka |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Best for | Variety, shopping, pop culture, museums | Food, nightlife, Kansai day trips |
| Short trip fit | Strong for 3 to 5 days | Strong for 3 to 4 days |
| First-time ease | Huge choice, more planning needed | Easier pacing, simpler base |
| Day trips | Yokohama, Kamakura, Hakone | Kyoto, Nara, Kobe |
| Watch-outs | Distances can be larger than expected | Popular areas can feel very busy |

## Best Fit

Tokyo is better for travelers who want maximum variety and do not mind planning by neighborhood. Osaka is better for travelers who want a food-first trip with easy side trips and less pressure to cover everything.
